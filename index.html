What a typical session looks like

1) Protect the body

Warm-up, mobility, movement prep, safe landing / turning mechanics.

2) Build athletic tools

Speed starts, agility patterns, balance control, core + lower-body strength basics.

3) Transfer to football

Reaction games, scanning habits, quick release, change-of-direction under pressure.

4) Mental mechanics (simple)

Reset routines, “lesson vs noise”, and accountability cues kids can actually use.

The Method: Physical + Mental

Your child doesn’t just need fitness — they need control. Control of body position, breathing, decisions, and emotions. We teach it in a way kids understand.

Physical pillars

Strength foundations (core/hips/legs), agility & deceleration, balance, coordination, and speed mechanics. Built for football — not bodybuilding.

Mental mechanics (no cringe)

The aim is simple: keep the lesson, drop the noise. We practise “reset” tools so kids don’t spiral after mistakes — they stay coachable and consistent.

Progress without pressure

We track progress with simple markers (not labels): movement quality, effort, consistency, and confidence under pressure. Kids improve best when they feel safe enough to try hard.

FAQ

What should my child bring?

Trainers/boots as appropriate, water, warm layers, and a good attitude. We’ll handle the structure.

Is this safe for kids?

Sessions include warm-up and movement prep every time. We scale drills by age/ability and prioritise technique before intensity.

My child is low confidence — will they cope?

Yes. We use simple cues, praise effort, and give “reset” routines so mistakes don’t become meltdowns. Confidence grows through small wins.

Do you do match coaching?

This is football fitness and performance coaching. If you want team coaching add-ons (warmups, speed blocks, mini-workshops), enquire.
